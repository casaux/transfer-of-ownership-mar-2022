With more drones in Australian skies, mandatory drone registration and accreditation are on the way to help make ﬂying safer and more enjoyable for all

Registration

Drones and model aircraft that weigh more than 250 g will need to be registered.

Registration is quick and easy and should only take a few minutes to complete online, through the myCASA portal.

Registration lasts for 12 months and fees may vary depending on how you intend to use your drone.

If your drone is lost or stolen, registration may help in returning it to you if it's found.

You need to register your drone or remotely piloted aircraft if you’re ﬂying at or for work, regardless of its weight.

Model aircraft flying at CASA-approved model airfields do not need to be registered if they will only be flown at that field.

How do I register my drone?

  • Create a a myCASA account account and verify your identity online. You can now do this using your Digital Identity, see below for further information
  • Apply for or link your aviation reference number (ARN) to your myCASA account
  • Provide drone details including make, model, serial number, weight and type of drone.

Some drones don’t need to be registered. This applies if:

  • you’re flying your drone exclusively indoors
  • you don’t intend to fly your drone
  • your drone weighs 250 g or less (and it’s not being flown for or at work).



What is Digital Identity?

A Digital Identity makes it easier to prove who you are online. It’s like the 100-point ID check, but on your smart device. Your Digital Identity makes accessing government services simpler, safer and more secure. Currently, myGovID is the app you will need to access government services using a Digital Identity.


Accreditation

New rules mean if you're flying a drone or model aircraft that weighs more than 250 g, you'll need to get accredited to show that you understand the drone safety rules.

Accreditation consists of a short video followed by a quiz to test your knowledge. You can complete it online, through the myCASA portal.

Accreditation is free, lasts for three years and provides you with an accreditation for ﬂying and supervising others. Children younger than 16 must be supervised by an accredited adult (aged 18 or older).

If you pass the quiz, you'll automatically receive your accreditation certificate — you'll be able to download and print it through myCASA.

If you fail, you'll be able to keep trying until you pass. There will be no limit on how many times you can attempt it, but you'll need to pass before you can ﬂy your drone.

By following the rules, you're helping to promote the safe and accountable use of drones in Australia.

Model aircraft association members, flying at CASA-approved model airfields, will not need to get accredited if they will only be flying at that field.

To get accredited, you will need:

  • to be at least 16 years old
  • a myCASA account
  • proof of identification – Australian passport, Australian birth certificate, Australian citizenship certificate, foreign passport or ImmiCard
  • an aviation reference number (ARN) – you can apply online, via your myCASA account.

Remember, if you're flying for fun, the drone safety rules apply now but you don't need to get accredited yet. Subscribe for the latest news and we'll let you know
